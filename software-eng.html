Software Engineering

TERMS DEFINITIONS
Software Engineering Body of Knowledge concerned with building reliable software systems that satisfy the requirements of users
Integrated Development Environment (IDE) Combine tools for developing software into a single, integrated package.
Association for Computing Machinery (ACM)
    Purpose of ACM: Brings together computing educators, researchers, and professionals to inspire dialogue, share resources, and address the fields challenges.
    Benefits of ACM Membership: Communications of the ACM, online books, courses, videos, and webinars through the learning the ACM learning center, option to subsribe and search to digital library, free year subscriptions, discounts, free email forwarding, and voting for ACM officers.
    Student ACM Membership: Online courses, books and videos, custom collection of courses, ability to search DL, online subscription, quick takes, newsletter, ACM magazines, free email forwarding address, special discounts, and participation in Distinguished Speakers Program.
Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE)
    Purpose of IEEE: Foster technological innovation and excellence for the benefit of humanity.
    Benefits of IEEE Membership: Join a community of over 420,000 technology and engineering professionals united by a common desire to continously learn, interact, collaborate, and innovate.
    Students of IEEE Membership: Earn valuable scholarships and grants to help get ahead in your education.
Software Life Cycle: Development phase Development stage includes analysis, design, implementation,and testing.
    Requirement Analysis: Provides a description of the proposed software product.
    Design: Involves creating a plan for the construction of the proposed system.
    Implementation: Involves the actual writing of programs, creation of data files and development databases.
    Testing: Process of debugging programs and confirming that the final software product is compatible with the software requirements specification.
Modular Implementation Seperating a programs functions into independent pieces or building blocks, each containing all the parts needed to execute a single aspect of functionality.
Unified Modeling Language (UML) Has been developed with the object-oriented paradigm in mind.
Use Case Interactions between the system and its users are represented as ovals and users of the system.
Use Case Diagram Attempts to capture the image of the proposed system from the users point of view.
Data Relationships Associations between tables that are created using join statements to retrieve data.
    One to One: Exemplified by the association between patients and occupied private rooms in that each patient is asscoaited with only one room, and each room is only associated with each patient.
    One to Many: Exemplified by the association between physicians and patients in that one physician is associated with many patients and each patient is associated with one primary association.
    Many to Many: Would occur if we included consulting physicians in the physician-patient relationship, then each physician can be associated with many patients, and each patient could be associated with many physicians.
Design Pattern Predeveloped model for solving a recurring problem in software design.
Software Quality Assurance (SQA) In charge of overseeing and enforcing the quality control systems adopted by the orginazation.
Software Testing Process of evaluating and verifying that a software product or application does what its supposed to do.
    Glass Box Testing: Software tester is aware of the interior structure of the software and uses this knowledge when designing the test.
    Black Box Testing: Refers to test that do not depend on knowledge of the softwares interior composition.
    Beta Testing: Preliminary version of the software is given to a segment of the intended audience with the goal of learning how the software performs in real life situations before the final version is solidified and released to the market.
    Alpha Testing: Similiar to black box testing that is performed at the developers site.
Documentation Information that describes the product to the people who develop, deploy and use it.
    User Documentation: Explains the features of the software and describe how to use them.
    System Documentation: To describe the softwares internal composition so that software can be maintained later in its life cycle.
    Technical Documentation: To describe how a software system should be installed and serviced.
Intellectual Property Law Legal efforts to provide ownership which is based on the well established principles of copyright and patent law.
    Software License: Legal agreement between the owner and the user of a software product that grants the user certain permissions to use the product without transferring ownership rights to the intellectual property.
    Software Disclaimer: States the limitations of their liability.
    Best defenses against Software Liability Claims is to apply sound software engineering principles during the softwares development,to use a level of care compatible with the softwares application, and to produce and maintain records that validate these endeavors.
